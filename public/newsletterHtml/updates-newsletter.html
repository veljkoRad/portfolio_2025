Fed Interest Rate Decision

The Federal Reserve meeting is today! Markets anticipate a much-needed pause in the interest rate hiking cycle, with markets forecasting a 95.3% chance of it happening, up from 75.9% after the US reported weaker-than-expected CPI data. With the S&P 500 officially entering into a new bull market, the positive sentiment and the expected relief from a potential pause in Federal Reserve interest rates hike could push US Indices to rally. Additionally, slowing rates could weaken the US dollar and increase commodity prices.

ECB Interest Rate Decision

The European Central Bank is making moves, too, with market expectation for a rate hike of 25 basis points. Despite inflation in the Eurozone moving lower over recent periods, the ECB has stated that it believes inflation is still too high. With Germany's CPI inflation showing a significant fall to 6.1% from the previous 7.2%, the inflation situation gets even more complex, especially with Germany facing a possible recession. Further rate hikes could strengthen the Euro but may negatively pressure European Indexes like the Ger40.

Chinese Industrial Production

Keep an eye on Chinese Industrial Production reports coming out tomorrow! While the market forecasts that the reports will show a slight decline, the previous three prints have demonstrated steady growth in industrial production in China. But despite the forecasted decline, a positive surprise could impact palladium prices. Additionally, Chinese crude oil imports remained strong, with China importing its third-highest crude oil volume throughout May. Should industrial production print a positive surprise, crude oil prices could move higher on anticipated demand spikes for crude oil use in industrial production.

What to expect this month?

 

With the current economic landscape with US Indices showing signs of a continued rally and the potential pause in Federal Reserve actions, optimism remains high. According to Vanda Research, equities are seeing an average of $1.36 billion of cash inflows per day from retail traders, some of the highest inflows in 2023. Additionally, commodities could see upward pricing pressures due to the weakened US dollar. The bullish turnaround could be sustained, at least until the Federal Reserve offers more guidance on future rates.
